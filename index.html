Who is Schrödinger's Cat?

Schrödinger's cat is not a real historical cat. It is a famous thought experiment created by physicist Erwin Schrödinger in 1935 to explain how strange quantum mechanics can be when applied to everyday objects.

Alive + Not Alive (before observation) Superposition Wavefunction Collapse

The Story (Simple Version)

  1. A sealed box contains a cat, a tiny amount of radioactive material, a detector, and poison.
  2. If one atom decays, the detector triggers and releases poison.
  3. Quantum rules say the atom can be both decayed and not decayed before measurement.
  4. So, in theory, the cat is in a blend of states (alive and not alive) until someone opens the box.

Why this matters

The point is not to harm a cat. Schrödinger used this setup to challenge interpretations of quantum mechanics and show how weird microscopic physics sounds at human scale.

Quick Facts

  • Created by: Erwin Schrödinger
  • Year: 1935
  • Type: Thought experiment
  • Main concept: Superposition and measurement
